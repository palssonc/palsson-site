Research

Working Papers

Early Experiments in Soft Power: State-Building in Haiti, 1915-34

with Christian Fignole

We use intelligence reports from the U.S. occupation of Haiti from 1915 to 1934 to show that American officials believed their state-building initiatives were persuading the Haitian elite to support the occupation.

Incomplete Property Rights and Farm Size: Evidence from Haiti

with Parker Sonnenberg

We demonstrate a connection between Haiti's tradition of jointly owned properties and misallocation in agricultural plots, explaining a key feature of Haiti's underdevelopment.

Migration in the Early 20th Century Caribbean: Evidence from Dominican Residency Permits

with Brian Marein

We look at migration patterns to the Dominican Republic using new data on over 400,000 work permits.

Narrow Paths Out of Poverty and Educational Demand: Evidence from Dominican Baseball

with Brian Marein

While many believe that professional baseball recruiting in the Dominican Republic has hurt education, we find no evidence that it decreased educational attainment.
