Scholarship with a long historical lens

Craig Palsson

Economic Historian, Development Economist, YouTuber

I am an associate professor of economics in the Huntsman School of Business at Utah State University. My research examines how historical political economy shapes modern underdevelopment, with a special focus on the economic history of Haiti.

I also create Market Power, a YouTube channel about economics, institutions, and the forces that shape the modern world.

Research profile

Questions that connect archives to present-day development

Economic history of Haiti

Much of my work studies how political instability, land institutions, and state capacity have shaped Haiti’s development path across the twentieth century.

Institutions and incentives

I study how incomplete property rights, government reach, and policy interventions affect economic behavior and long-run outcomes.

Applied contemporary questions

Another branch of my work looks at modern technology and transportation, including smartphones, ride-hailing, and public transit.
